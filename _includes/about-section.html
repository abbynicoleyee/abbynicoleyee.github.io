In my last life I did physics. Some time in 2017 I sat down in lab and, instead of aligning the laser, wrote a story. A few months later I published a sci-fi story, instead of a paper, in Nature. Then I started writing poetry, too, and it's all downhill from there.

I like writing about women and sharp things, and I put them in my poetry collection, Goddess Bandit of the Thousand Hands. I like my late-night second thoughts and illusions, optical or otherwise, like my prose chapbook, Hard Mother, Spider Mother, Soft Mother. And sometimes I close my eyes and try to remember what it was like to not know English, and I put those thoughts into the poems in AMNESIA.

I hope you'll stay a while and look around.
